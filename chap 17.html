üî∏ What is Poverty?

  • 'Comprehensive Poverty' is a situation where a large number of people are deprived of basic life requirements like food, clothes, shelter, education, and health services.
  • People living in this condition are called 'poor'.

üî∏ People Living Below Poverty Line (BPL) - Features

üìå Feature üìù Description
Food Insecurity Unable to get two meals a day. Suffer from malnutrition.
Lack of Shelter No permanent dwelling; forced to live in shabby slums.
Low Income Income is less than the determined expected income.
Low Life Expectancy Life span is less than the National Average.
Illiteracy Generally illiterate and lack information about government schemes.
Child Labour Children take up responsibility for the family instead of studying.

üî∏ The Concept of Poverty Line

  • First propounded by Byod Orr (Director of WHO).
  • It's a benchmark based on expenditure for food, clothing, education, health, and intake of calories.
  • This line is not static; it changes with time, location, and circumstances.

üî∏ Types of Poverty

‚ñ™Ô∏è Absolute Poverty

  • People who cannot afford basic necessities like food grains, pulses, and milk at the lowest market rates.

‚ñ™Ô∏è Relative Poverty

  • Income of one group is significantly lower than other groups in society. E.g., Person A with ‚Çπ10,000 income is relatively poor compared to Person B with ‚Çπ20,000. This is common in developed countries.

üî∏ Poverty Statistics and Data

üìä Poverty in India at a Glance

  • Planning Commission (2011-12): Poverty line set at per capita monthly expenditure of ‚Çπ816 (rural) and ‚Çπ1000 (urban).
  • Poverty Rate: Reduced from 29.8% (2009-10) to 21.9% (2011-12).
  • Total Poor (2011-12): 26.93 crore people (21.65 crore in rural areas, 5.28 crore in urban areas).
  • World Bank (2012): International poverty line at 1.90 US$ per day.
  • State-wise Scenario:
    • Poorest State: Chhattisgarh (36.93%)
    • Lowest Poverty: Goa (5.09%)
    • Gujarat: 16.63%

üî∏ "The Poor Live in Wealthy India"

  • This phrase highlights the paradox that India is rich in natural resources, but a large part of its population is poor due to lack of skills, education, and defective planning, which prevents the optimal utilization of these resources for public welfare.

üî∏ Reasons for Poverty

  • üåæ Insufficient development in agriculture & irrigation.
  • üßë‚Äçüè≠ Lack of alternative employment apart from farming.
  • üìö Lack of knowledge, skills, and education in rural areas.
  • üí∏ Unproductive expenditure due to social customs and traditions.
  • üìâ Collapse of cottage and small-scale industries after economic reforms.
  • üìà Population growth leading to increased labor supply and unemployment.

üî∏ Five-Pronged Strategy

  • (1) Industrial Development: Post-independence focus on heavy industries ("Gareebi Hatao") was an "optimistic bubbler" as benefits didn't trickle down.
  • (2) Taxation & PDS: High taxes on luxury goods and providing essential commodities to the poor through Fair Price Shops (FPS).
  • (3) Agricultural Reforms: Implemented land ceiling acts to improve conditions for farm laborers.
  • (4) Promoting Agro-based & Cottage Industries: Stressed on labor-intensive industries to increase employment.
  • (5) Improving Infrastructure: Focused on education, health, irrigation, and communication to curb migration to cities.

üî∏ Poverty Alleviation Programmes (PAP)

‚ñ™Ô∏è Main Objective: Provide direct financial benefits and employment to improve the economic condition of the poor.

üìå Programme Name üìù Key Feature & Description
Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojna (PMKSY) Irrigation for All: Aims to provide water to every field using drip irrigation and check dams to save farmers from debt.
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojna (PMFBY) Crop Insurance: Provides economic support to farmers against crop loss due to natural calamities.
E-Nam Yojna Online Market: An online platform for farmers to sell produce directly, avoiding brokers and getting better prices.
Gramoday se Bharat Uday Farmer-centric Schemes: Provides help for fencing, cattle shelters, and finance for mechanization to prevent losses and debt.
MAA Annapurna Yojna Food Security: Provides 35 kg of food grains to the absolute poor and 5 kg at subsidized rates to the middle class in Gujarat.
MANREGA Guaranteed Employment: Guarantees 100 days of wage employment per year to rural households for unskilled manual work.
Mission Mangalam Women Empowerment: Organizes women into Self-Help Groups (Sakhi Mandals) and provides skill training for self-employment.
Jyoti Gramodyog Vikas Yojna Rural Self-Employment: Provides economic help to unemployed youth to set up industries, linked with "Start up India".

üî∏ What is Unemployment?

  • A person who is an adult (15-60 years), is willing and able to work at the prevalent market wage rate, but does not get a job.

üß† Mnemonic for Types of Unemployment

S. F. S. D. I. E.

"Some Friends Should Definitely Investigate Education"

üî∏ Types of Unemployment

üìå Type üìù Description
Seasonal Unemployment for a few months in a year, especially in agriculture due to lack of irrigation.
Frictional Temporary unemployment when workers change jobs or new technology is introduced.
Structural Mismatch between job seekers' skills and jobs available, due to economic structural changes.
Disguised More people work in a job than required. Removing some workers doesn't affect production (common in agriculture).
Industrial Unemployment due to changes in the industrial sector (e.g., recession, technological shifts).
Educated A person has a secondary education or degree but remains unemployed.

üî∏ Reasons for Unemployment

  • üìà High rate of population growth.
  • üìñ Focus on theoretical knowledge over practical/technical skills.
  • üöú Lack of alternative employment during the off-season in agriculture.
  • üìâ Weak condition of cottage and small-scale industries.
  • üíº Lack of courage and investment to start new businesses.

E. Efforts to Reduce Unemployment üõ†Ô∏è

üî∏ Government Initiatives

  • High Economic Growth: Aiming for 10% annual economic development.
  • Skill Development: Ambitious programmes like "Make in India," "Skill India," and "Digital India" to enhance youth skills.
  • Entrepreneurship: Promoting new businesses through schemes like "Start up India" with low-interest loans.
  • Rural Focus: Creating employment in villages through construction, agriculture-related activities (poultry, dairy), and afforestation to reduce migration.
  • Employment Exchanges: Centres that act as a bridge between the unemployed and employers, providing information and guidance. The helpline number is 1800-425-1514.

üî∏ World Labour Market üåç

  • Refers to the migration of labor between countries for jobs, business, or education.
  • Brain Drain: The migration of highly skilled and intelligent people from their home country in search of better opportunities, causing a loss of talent.
  • Globalization has increased the demand for professionals skilled in IT, communication, biotechnology, etc.
