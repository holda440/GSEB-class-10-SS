A. What is Human Development? ü§î

üî∏ Core Concept

  • Human development is a process of enlarging people's freedoms and opportunities and improving their well-being.
  • It's a shift from viewing development purely in economic terms (like GDP) to a more holistic, people-centered approach.
  • The ultimate goal is to create an environment where people can lead long, healthy, and creative lives.

üî∏ The Four Pillars of Human Development

Mnemonic: E.S.P.E

Equality - Equal access to opportunities for all.

Stability - Ensuring life's achievements are not threatened.

Production - Enhancing human productivity and participation.

Empowerment - Giving people the power to make choices.

üî∏ Human Development Means:

  • Feature: Self-determined Life
    Description: The ability to lead a successful and creative life according to one's own interests, skills, and wisdom.
  • Feature: Health & Longevity
    Description: Living a healthy, fit, and long life.
  • Feature: Knowledge
    Description: Acquiring knowledge through information and education.
  • Feature: Qualitative Lifestyle
    Description: Access to natural resources, improved health conditions, and personal & social security.
  • Feature: Human Rights
    Description: The ability to utilize inherent human rights.

B. The Human Development Index (HDI) üìà

üî∏ Origin and Purpose

  • The concept was developed by economist Mahbub ul Haq and propounded by Nobel laureate Amartya Sen.
  • The first Human Development Report was published by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) in 1990.
  • HDI is a summary measure for assessing long-term progress in three basic dimensions of human development.

üî∏ The Three Indices Explained

1. Life Expectancy Index (LEI): Measures health and longevity. It is based on the life expectancy at birth. In the 2015 report, the global average life expectancy was 68 years.
2. Education Index (EI): Measures access to knowledge. It has two components:

- Mean Years of Schooling (MYS): Average years of education received by people aged 25 and older. (India 2015: 5.4 years)

- Expected Years of Schooling (EYS): Total number of years of schooling a child of school-entering age can expect to receive. (India 2015: 11.7 years)

3. Income Index (II): Measures a decent standard of living. It uses Gross National Income (GNI) per capita, adjusted for Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) in US dollars to account for price differences between countries. (India 2015 GNI: $5,497)

üî∏ India's HDI Journey

  • 2015 Report: India ranked 130th out of 188 countries with an HDI of 0.609, placing it in the 'Medium Human Development' category.
  • Progressive Improvement: India's HDI has shown consistent growth: 0.428 (1990) ‚ûù 0.496 (2000) ‚ûù 0.586 (2010) ‚ûù 0.609 (2015).

E. Government Initiatives for Empowerment üí™

üî∏ National Level Schemes & Policies

  • 1975: Declared "Women's Year" by the United Nations.
  • 1992: Establishment of the National Commission for Women.
  • 2001: National Policy for Empowerment of Women was set up.
  • 2002: Celebrated as "Women Empowerment Year".
  • 181 Abhayam Helpline: A helpline (started in Gujarat) for women facing violence, providing security and guidance.

üî∏ Schemes by Gujarat Government for Women & Girls

  • üìö 'Vidyalaxmi Bond': Financial aid for girls from poor families for primary/secondary education.
  • üö≤ 'Saraswati Sadhna Yojna': Free cycles for girls to travel to school.
  • üçé 'Sabla Yojna': Provides nutritional food and skill development for adolescent girls.
  • üíº Reservation: 33% reservation for women in government jobs, increased to 50% in local self-government institutions.
  • ü§ù 'Mission Mangalam': Economic help to women through Self-Help Groups (Sakhi Mandals).
  • ü§∞ 'E-mamta Programme': Uses mobile tech to register and track pregnant women, ensuring regular checkups and reducing maternal mortality.
  • üëß 'Beti Bachhao, Beti Padhao': A campaign to fight gender discrimination and promote girls' education.
  • üè• 'Chiranjivi Yojna': Provides free medical services (medicines, tests, operations) to pregnant women from SC/ST communities.
