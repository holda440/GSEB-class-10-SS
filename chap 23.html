23 - Road Safety and Motorists

A. Duties of Drivers (Motorists) and Riders üöó
  • A driver must be in a fit condition to operate a vehicle.
    • Physically and mentally fit.
    • Not under any kind of intoxication (e.g., alcohol, drugs).
  • Maintain full concentration and avoid distractions.
    • Keep a constant eye on the road and traffic.
    • Do not get distracted by passengers, animals, goods, or phone calls.
  • Ensure the safety of all road users.
    • Drive with special care for vulnerable road users: pedestrians üö∂, cyclists üö¥, children, and the elderly.
    • Take care of your own safety and that of others when boarding or alighting.
    • Ensure all passengers (and self) wear seat belts.
    • Motorcycle riders and pillion riders MUST wear a helmet.
  • Maintain the vehicle in good condition.
    • Do not drive a vehicle with dangerous defects.
    • Before starting, check mirrors, tyres, and brakes.
  • Follow proper road conduct.
    • Do not stop in a way that obstructs traffic.
    • Do not honk unnecessarily, especially in quiet zones.
    • Be careful when opening doors to avoid collisions with passing traffic.
Remember the driver's duty with SAFE

S - Safety First (for self, passengers, and vulnerable users)
A - Alert & Attentive (no distractions, no intoxication)
F - Fit to Drive (physically and mentally)
E - Equipment Check (mirrors, tyres, brakes, safety gear)

C. Lane Traffic Rules üõ£Ô∏è
  • Always drive in your designated lane.
  • Generally, light, medium-speed, and heavy vehicles should use the leftmost lane.
  • Only change lanes when it is safe, after giving a proper signal in advance.
  • Do not cross a continuous white or yellow line.
  • When a broken line is next to a solid line, vehicles on the side of the broken line may cross to overtake, returning to their lane immediately after.

D. Intersections & Roundabouts üîÑ
  • Slow down when approaching a crossroad, junction, or roundabout.
  • Give priority to vehicles coming from your right.
  • Use signals when changing lanes within or exiting a roundabout.

Importance of Helmet

  • During an accident, a helmet absorbs the impact, reducing the force on the skull.
  • It prevents the brain from striking the inside of the skull, which can cause severe injury or immediate death.

Do you know? üß†

  • Section-129 (Motor Vehicle Act): Makes it mandatory for every two-wheeler driver and pillion rider (above 4 years) to wear a standard helmet.
  • Section-194 (D): Not wearing a helmet is punishable by a fine and can lead to a 3-month suspension of the driving licence.

Importance of Seat Belt

  • In a crash, a seat belt prevents the occupant from colliding with the car's interior (steering wheel, windscreen).
  • It is the most effective way to prevent fatal injuries in a road accident.
  • The "Child Restraint System" is crucial for protecting children.

Do you know? üß†

  • Section-194 (B) (Motor Vehicle Act): Driving or travelling in a motorcar without wearing a seat belt is a punishable offence.
F. One-Mark Facts for Quick Review ‚ö°
  • 16 Years: Minimum age for a licence for a two-wheeler up to 50cc without gear (MCWoG).
  • 18 Years: Minimum age for a Light Motor Vehicle (LMV) licence.
  • 20 Years: Minimum age for a heavy vehicle licence (must have LMV licence for 1+ year).
  • Priority at Roundabouts: Give way to traffic from the right.
  • Main Lane for Heavy Vehicles: The leftmost lane.
  • Section 129: Mandates wearing a helmet.
  • Section 194 (B): Punishment for not wearing a seat belt.
  • Section 194 (D): Punishment for not wearing a helmet, including licence suspension.
