üíú Chapter 3: Cultural Heritage of India: Sculpture and Architecture üíú

India's rich cultural heritage is world-famous, especially its unique sculpture and architecture. Ancient Indian town planning (like Indus Valley) was remarkably advanced!

üî∂ 1. Understanding the Basics üî∂
A. Sculpture üóø

Definition: The art of carving feelings on stone, wood, or metal using a hammer and chisel. It involves shaping figures or designs with facial expressions, either in round or relief.

Emoji Key: üóø = Sculpture!

B. Architecture üèóÔ∏è

Definition: The art of construction.

Sanskrit Word: ‚ÄòVastu‚Äô (renowned term).

Includes: Constructing buildings, houses, towns, wells, forts, minarets, temples, mosque and tombs.

üíö 2. Ancient Indian Town Planning üíö

India had expertise in town planning. Excavated towns show a common division:

Town Division Flowchart:

[Visual Flowchart for PDF: Ancient Town leads to three branches]

Ancient Town
    |--> 1. Fort of Rulers / Citadel
    |--> 2. Houses of Administrative Officers - Upper Part
    |--> 3. Residences of Common People - Lower Part
  • Key Features Noted:
    • Forts for rulers on the upper part.
    • Upper part: safe, protected, houses with 2-5 rooms.
    • Lower part: houses mainly of handmade bricks.
A. Mohan-Jo-Daro (Sindhi: "Mound of the Dead Men") üèôÔ∏è
  • Discovered: 1922, by Rakhal Das Banerji & Dayaram Sahni.
  • Guidance: Sir John Marshal & Colonel Meke.
  • Location: Larkhan district (now in Pakistan).
  • Meaning: 'The heap of the deads'.
  • Key Features:
    1. Construction:
      • Best town planning.
      • Houses on high plinths (protection from floods/dampness).
      • Rich: Double-storeyed, 5-7 rooms.
      • Poor: Single-storeyed, 2-3 rooms.
      • Town fortified. Entrances on side lanes, not main roads.
      • Every house: storehouse, kitchen, bathroom. Proper doors/windows (airy, ventilated).
    2. Roads üõ£Ô∏è:
      • Main feature! 9.75 meters wide.
      • Small link roads crossed major ones at right angles.
      • Wide enough for many vehicles.
      • Pits on roadside (suggest lamp posts).
      • Straight main roads, no turns (specialty of ancient times).
      • Two main roads: North-South & East-West, crossing at right angles.
    3. Drainage System üíß:
      • Unique feature! (Only other place: Crete, Mediterranean).
      • Drained dirty water out. Each house had a cesspit.
      • Planned, systematic, efficient (shows care for health & hygiene).
    4. Public Baths üõÄ:
      • Huge bath excavated.
      • Arrangement for fresh water in, dirty water out.
      • Possible hot water facility, changing rooms.
      • Used for festivals/religious ceremonies.
    5. Public Buildings üèõÔ∏è:
      • Ruins of 2 huge buildings (town hall, theatre, administrative office, or granary).
      • Barracks for soldiers also found.
B. Harappa üß±
  • Discovered: 1921, by Dayaram Sahni.
  • Guidance: Sir John Marshal & Colonel Meke.
  • Location: Montgomery district, Punjab (near Harappa).
  • Also known as: Harappan Civilization (as its remains found first here).
  • Other Sites: Rapar, Alamgirpur (UP), Kalibangan (Rajasthan), Lothal (Gujarat), Dholavira, etc.
  • Age: Copper-Stone Age (due to copper & stone weapons found).
  • Features: Systematic town planning, remarkable granaries & forts, people fond of ornaments.
C. Dholaveera (Gujarat) üèúÔ∏è
  • Description: Huge, systematic ancient town, contemporary to Harappan civilization.
  • Location: Khadirbet, Bhachau taluka (140 km from Bhuj).
  • Excavation: 1990 AD, under Ravindra Singh Bisht (ASI).
  • Features:
    • Palaces, main walls painted white.
    • Fortified walls (clay, stone, bricks) suggesting strong security.
    • Potable water & advanced water purification system.
D. Lothal (Gujarat) ‚öì
  • Location: Dholka taluka, Ahmedabad district (between Bhogavo & Sabarmati rivers), 18 km from Bay of Khambhat.
  • Unique Feature: Huge dockyard to facilitate ships at high tides (lower eastern part).
  • Evidence of Trade: Dockyard, storage shops show proof of exports & imports.
  • Significance: Rich & prosperous port of India. Pride for Gujarat & India.
üíô 3. Mauryan Art üíô
A. Stupa ‚ò∏Ô∏è
  • Definition: An oval-shaped construction under which remains of Lord Buddha's body were kept in a box.
  • Famous Ashokan Stupas (5): Sanchi, Sarnath, Berat, Nandangadh, Devanimori (Gujarat).
  • Other Constructions: Chaityas, Viharas, Maths.
  • Ashoka's Period: Golden period for Buddhist religion, sculpture & architecture.
  • Stupa of Sanchi (Madhya Pradesh):
    • Built during Mauryan Period.
    • Original: made of bricks, half the size of the present one. Precious specimen.
B. Parts of a Stupa (Linear Sketch) üó∫Ô∏è

[Visual Flowchart for PDF: Stupa with four parts]

Stupa
    |--> Harmika: Railing at the top
    |--> Medhi: Elevated circular path for pradakshina
    |--> Pradakshina Path: Slightly elevated circular path, worship place to right
    |--> Toran: Gateway with 2 high pillars & horizontal beam - entrance

Mnemonic for Stupa Parts: "Happy Monks Pray Through" (Harmika, Medhi, Pradakshina Path, Toran)

C. Pillar Inscriptions üìú
  • Made from single rock. Carved by Emperor Ashoka's religious order.
  • Polished so well they gleamed (shined).
  • Locations: Ambala, Meerut, Allahabad, Sarnath, Loriya (near Nandangadh), Sanchi, Kashi, Patna, Bodhivruksha (near Bodh-Gaya).
  • Script: Brahmi.
  • Pillar at Sarnath:
    • Best specimen of sculpture.
    • Four lions facing four directions (National Emblem).
    • Dharmachakras carved below lions (indicates triumph of religion - "Dharmachakra").
    • Also has sculptures of elephant, horse, bullock.
    • Chakra is in India's national flag.
D. Stone Inscriptions üìù
  • Carved by Ashoka's religious orders.
  • Excellent specimens along with wooden art and stone sculpting.
  • Beautiful arches engraved on doors.
  • Locations: Peshawar, Dehradun, Thane, Mumbai, Dhauli, Jaugada (Odisha), Chennai.
  • Gujarat: Foothill of Girnar (Junagadh), Palitana Jain temples, Hathisinh Jain temple (Ahmedabad, 1847 AD - Sanskrit & Gujarati).
üí† 4. Dravid Style of Art in South India üí†
  • Buddhist Stupas near Krishna & Godavari rivers (Satavahana kings).
  • Shapes: Semi-circle, oval, bell-shaped.
  • Best Specimens: Stupas in Nagarjunakonda & Amravati.
  • Chola kings adopted Dravid style extensively.
ü§é 5. Art of Gupta Period (Golden Period of Art!) ü§é
  • Flourished: Architecture, sculpture, painting, dancing, music.
  • Examples:
    • Temples: Parvati temple (Jabalpur), Bhumara Shiva temple (Nagoda), Eran's Narsinh temple (MP), Gopmandir (Jamnagar).
    • Structures: Stupas, Chaityas, Maths, Viharas, Flags, Pillars.
    • Sculptures: Buddha (Sarnath), Lord Vishnu (Mathura), Mahavir Swami, Udayagiri caves statues, Vishnu as Varah.
üíú 6. Cave Architecture üíú

Considered manmade places of beauty. üèûÔ∏è

A. Famous Indian Caves:
  • Ajanta & Ellora (Aurangabad)
  • Elephanta (Mumbai)
  • Udayagiri & Bagh (Gwalior)
  • Bhuvneshwari (Odisha)
  • Khandgiri & Nilgiri
  • Ashoka's cave inscriptions (Berber mountain, near Gaya) describe charity works.
  • Caves in Assam, Darjiling, Sudama cave (Bihar), Seeta cave.
B. Caves of Gujarat ‡§ó‡•Å‡§ú‡§∞‡§æ‡§§:
  1. Junagadh Caves:
    • Bavapyara caves: 3 layers, 16 caves (1st-2nd cent. AD).
    • Uperkot caves: Double-storeyed (end 2nd - early 4th cent. AD).
    • Khapra & Kediya (Kund caves): Floored caves, 20 pillars (3rd cent. AD).
  2. Khambhalida Caves (near Gondal): Discovered 1959 AD. 3 remarkable. Middle cave: Chaitya Gruh with stupa. Bodhisattva statues (2nd-3rd cent. AD).
  3. Talaja Cave (Bhavnagar): 30 caves. Ebhal Mandap (Sabhakhand) & Chaitya Gruh are best. Buddhist (3rd cent. AD).
  4. Sana Cave (Gir Somnath): Spread like bee-hive on Sana mountain.
  5. Dhank Cave (Rajkot): Dhank giri (early 4th cent. AD).
  6. Jhinjhuri Jhar (near Dhank): Buddhist caves (2nd cent. AD).
  7. Khapra-Kodiya Caves (Kachchh): 2 caves. Discovered by K.K. Shastri 1967 AD.
  8. Kaliya Dungar Caves (Bharuch): 3 caves. Best old Buddhist architecture. 11-ft high lion statue (2 bodies, 1 mouth, single rock).
‚ù§Ô∏è 7. Chariot Temples (Ratha Temples) ‚ù§Ô∏è
  • Unique feature of Pallava age (South India). Carved from a single rock.
  • Examples:
    • Kailashnath temple (Kanchi), Vaikuntha Perumal temple (Kanchi).
    • Mandapas & Chariot temples of Mahabalipuram (world-famous, rock-cut).
  • Named after Pandavas: Dharmaraja Ratha (biggest), Draupadi Ratha (smallest).
  • Emoji: üèõÔ∏è‚û°Ô∏èüöó (Temple turning into a chariot!)
üíõ 8. Temple Architecture üíõ
A. General Features:
  • High pedestal, pinnacle (shikhar), stairs.
  • Some flat with pradakshina path around 'Garbha Griha' (innermost part).
  • Early Examples: Bhoomara Shiva temple (Jabalpur), Larkhan temples (Bijapur), Copper Buddha statue (Nalanda/Sultangunj), Jain temples (Mathura).
  • Pallava Kings: Great contribution. Kanchi temples.
  • Chola Dynasty: Tanjavar (capital). Brihadeeshwarar Temple (by Rajraja Chola, 1st of its kind, ~200m high).
B. Architecture of Gopuram (Temple Entrance Gateways) üö™
  • Gopuram = Entrance.
  • Encouraged by Pandya rulers (South India).
  • High outer walls, beautifully decorated gates.
  • Artistic glory of Gopuram often more popular than the temple itself.
  • Examples: Gopurams of Kanchi & Madurai.
C. Parts of a Temple (Linear Sketch) üó∫Ô∏è

[Visual Flowchart for PDF: Temple with five parts]

Temple
    |--> Garbha Griha: Small, dark room for idol; 'Gabharo' in Gujarat
    |--> Gopuram: Southern temple gateway; pyramidal structure
    |--> Mandap: Big hall on pillars or area in front of main gate
    |--> Pinnacle/Shikhar: Pointed top of Garbh Griha; gold/brass plated
    |--> Viman: Semi-circle/sloping part of temple; many storeys, pyramid-like

Mnemonic for Temple Parts: "Great Gods Meet People via Vimanas" (Garbha Griha, Gopuram, Mandap, Pinnacle, Viman)

D. Key Temple Examples:
  • Sun Temple, Konark (Odisha): Chariot temple form. ‚òÄÔ∏èüê¥
  • Brihadeeshwarar Temple, Tanjavur: 13-storeyed Gopuram. Bronze Natraj statue.
  • Meenakshi Temple, Madurai: Huge, 4 main Gopurams.
  • Khajuraho Temples (Bundelkhand): Built by Chandela rulers. Finest temples, varied pinnacle styles.
üåø 9. Jain Temples (Derasar) üåø
  • Locations: Vaibhar (Rajgruha), Vipulachal, Ratnagiri, Udayagiri, Shramgiri (5 Jain temples).
  • Samet Shikharji (Bihar): "Madhuvan". Aadinath Bhagwan & 20 Tirthankars attained Nirvana.
  • Gujarat: Palitana, Shankheshwar (Panchasara).
  • Delwara (Mount Abu) & Ranakpur (Rajasthan):
    • Excellent construction, carving, artistic skill.
    • Delwara: 'Vimal Vasahi' (by minister Vimal Shah) & 'Luna Vasahi' (by minister Vastupal).
    • Made of white marble. Famous for minute, artistic sculpture. World-famous.
üîÜ 10. Sun Temple of Modhera (Gujarat) üîÜ
  • Location: Modhera, Mahesana District.
  • Built: 1026 AD, by Solanki King Bhimdev-I.
  • Features:
    • Gem in Sun God's crown; first sun ray falls on it, lighting up sanctum.
    • 12 different Sun God images, erotic sculptures.
    • Carving in Iranian school of Art style.
    • 108 small temples around an outside tank.
üçÇ 11. Medieval Architecture üçÇ
A. General Sultanate/Provincial Styles:
  • Includes: Mosques, minarets, royal palaces, bridges, inns.
  • Delhi Sultanate: Qutub-ud-din Aibak built Qutubminar, Quwwat-ul-Islam mosque, Adhai-Din-ka Jhopara (Ajmer).
  • Bengal: Adina Mosque (Pandua), Tomb of Jalal-ud-Muhammad Shah. Own special style.
  • Jaunpur: Atala Mosque (by Turky Sultans). Artistic grill, Indian designs (lotus).
  • Malwa: Mandu buildings. Impressive domes, balcony carvings. Tomb of Hoshang Shah (marble, Indian style).
  • Other Provinces (Vijaynagar, Bahmani, Kashmir): Dome of Bijapur, Bidar/Gulbargani buildings, Madrasa of Mahmud Gava, Hampi temples (Vithala Swami, Hazar Rama), Gopurams.
B. Architecture of Gujarat (Medieval):
  • Includes: Temples, mosques, viharas, maths, stupas, chaityas, cave-temples, Jain temples.
  • Also: Palaces, forts, domes, gates, kirtistambh (tower of fame), inns, wells, step-wells, lakes.
  • Temples (Gujarat): Bhadrakali, Geeta Mandir, Jagannath (Jamalpur), Ranchhodraiji (Dakor), Sun temple (Modhera), Hatkeshwar (Vadnagar), Ambaji, Somnath, Dwarka, Swaminarayan temples etc.
  • Mosques (Gujarat) üïå:
    • Jama Masjid (Ahmedabad): Near Teen Darwaja. By Sultan Ahmad Shah-I (1424 AD). 260 pillars, 15 domes.
    • Others: Sidi Saiyyad grill (minute carving), Roza of Sarkhej, Shaking towers (Ahmedabad), Mosque of Queen Sipri ('Mosque of Nageena'), Jama Masjid (Champaner).
  • Jain Temples (Gujarat): Hathi Sinh, Kumbhariyaji, Shankheshwar, Siddhgiri, Shetrunjya giri, Palitana.
  • Step-wells (Vav) of Gujarat üíß:
    • Adalaj Vav, Dada Hari ni Vav (Ahmedabad), Rani ki Vav (Patan), Step-well of Dabhoi. Splendid examples.
  • Other Structures (Gujarat): Fort of Bhadra, Teen Darwaza, Kankariya Lake (Ahmedabad), Rudra Mahalaya (Siddhpur), Sahastralinga Lake (Patan), Kirti Toran (Vadnagar).
C. Parts of a Mosque (Linear Sketch) üó∫Ô∏è

[Visual Flowchart for PDF: Mosque with six parts]

Mosque
    |--> Galiyara: Entry/Exit way
    |--> Kibla: Wall/Part of hall for Namaz, faces Kaba
    |--> Livan: Room with pillars
    |--> Maksura: End of mosque wall, separated by railing
    |--> Mahrab: Interior part of mosque wall, indicates direction to Mecca - West in India
    |--> Sahan: Campus for followers to gather for prayer

Mnemonic for Mosque Parts: "Go Kindly, Learn, Make Many Supplications" (Galiyara, Kibla, Livan, Maksura, Mahrab, Sahan)
