üî∏ What is Transportation?

  • The movement of people and goods from one place to another is called transportation.
  • It plays a crucial role in economic and physical progress, linking distant places and enabling national integration, industrialization, and urbanization.

üî∏ Evolution of Transportation

  • Early Man: Lived a wanderer's life, carrying things himself.
  • Post-Agriculture: Started a sedentary life, using animals (beasts of burden).
  • Modern Period: Widespread use of auto vehicles.

üî∏ Factors Affecting Transportation

  • Physical Factors: Location, climate, relief (terrain).
  • Cultural Factors: Technical development, economic growth, market & capital investments, political decisions.

üî∏ India's National Transportation System Ratio

Roadways - 83%
Railways - 9%
Airways - 6%
Waterways - 2%

Type of Road Features & Description
National Highway (NH) Connects state capitals, major cities, ports, and neighboring countries. Responsibility of the Central Government. NH-44 (Srinagar to Kanyakumari) is the longest.
State Highway (SH) Connects district headquarters with state capitals and national highways. Responsibility of State Governments.
District Roads Connects taluka headquarters and main villages with the district headquarters. Maintained by district panchayats.
Village (Approach) Roads Connects villages to other roads. Maintained by Gram Panchayat. The Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana aims to convert these into metalled roads.
Border Roads Constructed and maintained by the Border Road Organisation (BRO, est. 1960) in remote border areas for defence and protection.
Expressways Also called Drut Gati Marg. High-speed, multi-lane (4 or 6) highways with no obstructions (overbridges for crossings). Example: Ahmedabad-Vadodara Expressway. Toll tax is collected.

‚ú® 2. Railways üöÇ

üìå Key Features

  • India's largest national institution, ranking first in Asia and second in the world in railway network size.
  • Crucial for agriculture, industry, trade, national security, and cultural unity.
  • First railway in India: 1853, between Mumbai and Thane.

üìå Railway Gauges

  • Broad Gauge: The widest, now the standard.
  • Metre Gauge & Narrow Gauge: Being converted to broad gauge to save time and money.

üìå Important Facts

  • Konkan Railway: A marvel of engineering, built through mountainous regions with many tunnels.
  • Vivek Express: Longest railway route in India (Dibrugarh to Kanyakumari).
  • Metro Rail: Operational in major cities like Kolkata, Delhi, and Jaipur. Work started for Ahmedabad-Gandhinagar.

‚ú® 3. Waterways üö¢

üìå Advantages

  • The cheapest mode of transport as no construction or maintenance costs are incurred for the route itself.

üìå Types of Waterways

  • Internal Waterways: Using rivers and canals within the country. More developed in Assam, West Bengal, and Bihar.
  • Oceanic Waterways: Using the sea and oceans for transport along the coast and internationally.

üìå Oceanic Waterways & Ports

  • India has a long coastline of about 7516 km.
  • There are 13 major and about 200 minor ports.
  • Gujarat's Coastline: ~1600 km. Kandla is its largest port. Bhavnagar has an automatic lock-gate system.

‚ú® 4. Airways ‚úàÔ∏è

  • The speediest but also the costliest mode of transport.
  • Ideal for reaching distant, remote, and inaccessible areas (e.g., dense forests, mountains).
  • The Airport Authority of India manages 127 airports (15 international, 87 domestic, 25 civilian terminals).
  • Pavan Hans Helicopters Ltd. provides services to ONGC and the government.

‚ú® 5. Other Means of Transportation

üìå Pipelines

  • Used to transport liquids and gases like water, mineral oil, and natural gas.
  • Key pipeline: Naharkatia (Assam) to Barauni (Bihar); Salaya (Gujarat) to Mathura (UP).

üìå Ropeways

  • Connect summits in mountainous regions to transport goods and passengers.
  • Locations in India: Darjeeling, Kullu-Manali, Haridwar.
  • Locations in Gujarat: Pavagadh, Saputara, Ambaji. Work has started at Girnar (Junagadh).

B. COMMUNICATION üìû ‚úâÔ∏è üõ∞Ô∏è

Communication is the arrangement to send or collect information from one place to another. It is vital for daily life, disaster relief, and national unity.

Types of Communication Systems
Individual Communication
‚ÜîÔ∏è
Mass Communication
Communication Type Features & Description
Individual Communication One-to-one communication. Effective gadgets include the internet, smartphones, e-mail, enabling e-commerce, currency exchange, and social media connectivity.
Mass Communication Communicating with a large audience simultaneously.
  • Print Media: Newspapers, magazines, pamphlets.
  • Electronic Media: Akashwani (radio) and Doordarshan (television). Prasar Bharti is the autonomous body.
Satellite Communication Artificial satellites like INSAT (Indian National Satellite) and IRS (Indian Remote Sensing Satellite) are used for weather forecasting, disaster warnings, telecasts, and research. Launched by vehicles like PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle).

C. TRADE üíπ

India's diversity in climate, resources, and industrial output necessitates trade.

Types of Trade
Internal Trade
‚ÜîÔ∏è
International Trade
Trade Type Description
Internal Trade Trade of commodities within a country, between different states. E.g., Punjab exports wheat to other states and imports salt from Gujarat.
International Trade Trade between different countries of the world.
  • Trade Balance: The difference between exports and imports.
    • Positive: Exports > Imports (Increases foreign exchange).
    • Negative: Imports > Exports.
  • India's Imports: Petroleum, mineral oil, machinery, pearls, gemstones.
  • India's Exports: Iron ore, engineering goods (cycles, fans), computer software, chemicals, leather goods, textiles, tea, coffee.
