Chapter 7 - Preservation of Our Heritage

India holds a unique place in the world due to its remarkable contributions to knowledge, science, culture, and art. This rich and inimitable heritage is our identity and guide, making its preservation an utmost necessity for the present and future generations.

MNEMONIC for Remembering Key Bodies/Acts:
"Brave Indians Always Work"
  • Bombay Natural History Society (1883)
  • Indian Board of Wildlife (1952)
  • Ancient Monuments Act (1958)
  • Wildlife Protection Act (1972)
  • Protection of Wildlife
    Feature: Indian Board of Wildlife (1952)
    Constituted to protect wildlife, it provides material for conservation and suggests the development of national parks, sanctuaries, and gardens.
    Feature: Wildlife Protection Act (1972)
    Gave a firm legal status to national parks, sanctuaries, and bio-reserves to stop the destruction of vegetation and wildlife.
  • Protection of Monuments
    Feature: Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act (1958)
    Aimed at preserving antique artistic works, religious places, and historical sites. It mandates government permission for any archaeological excavation, stopping illegal digging.
    Feature: Department of Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)
    Responsible for the maintenance and preservation of over 5000 monuments. A key principle is to maintain monuments in a way that retains their original shape, size, and condition.
    Example: Successfully shifted the Sangameshwar and Papnasham temples in Andhra Pradesh to save them from submergence due to the Nagarjun Sagar Project.
