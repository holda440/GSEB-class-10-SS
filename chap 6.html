  • Mahabalipuram
    • Location & Patronage: 60 km from Chennai, Tamilnadu. Built by Narsinh Varman-I (Pallava dynasty).
    • Features: Famous for 7 Chariot Temples (Pancha Rathas), rock sculptures, and seashore temples.
  • Temples at Khajuraho
    • Location & Patronage: Chattarpur, Madhya Pradesh. Built by Chandela Rajputs (950-1050 AD).
    • Architecture: Naagar style, built with granite stone. The 64 Yoginis temple is the main temple.

  • Hampi
    • Location & History: Karnataka. Capital of the Vijaynagar kingdom. Golden period under Krishnadevaraya.
    • Features: Known for huge, magnificent pillars carved from single stones, Vithala temple, and Virupaksha temple.
  • Qutub Minar
    • Location & Dimensions: Delhi. India's tallest stone minaret at 72.5 meters.
    • Builders: Started by Qutubbudin Aibak, completed by his son-in-law Iltutmish.
  • Taj Mahal
    • Location & Builder: Agra, UP. Built by Shah Jahan in memory of his queen Mumtaz Mahal.
    • Construction: Took 22 years (1631-1653 AD). One of the Seven Wonders of the World.
  • Red Fort (Delhi)
    • Builder & Date: Built by Shah Jahan in 1638 AD.
    • Key Structures Inside: Diwan-e-Aam, Diwan-e-Khas, Rang Mahal, and the famous Peacock Throne (taken by Nadir Shah).
  • Fatehpur Sikri
    • Founder & Purpose: Founded by Akbar as his capital city, in memory of Sufi saint Shaikh Salim Chisti.
    • Key Structures: Buland Darwaza (50m high), Jama Masjid, Palace of Jodhabai, Panch Mahal.

D. HERITAGE SITES OF GUJARAT ü¶Å

  • Ancient Cities (Indus Valley)
    • Dholaveera: Located in Kutch, known for ideal town planning and bead-making factories.
    • Lothal: An ancient port city and a major center of trade and commerce.
  • Key Cities & Monuments
    • Ahmedabad: A historical city with sites like Sidi Saiyed Jali, Jama Masjid, and Shaking Minarets.
    • Patan: Famous for 'Rani ni vav' (Queen's Step-well), a UNESCO World Heritage site built by Queen Udaymati.
    • Champaner-Pavagadh: A UNESCO site known for its blend of Hindu-Muslim architecture, including the Jama Masjid.
    • Junagadh: Home to Ashoka's rock edicts, Buddhist caves, and the tomb of Bahauddin Vazir.

üìù One-Mark Facts for Quick Revision

  • Ajanta Caves were rediscovered in 1819 AD by John Smith.
  • Ellora has a total of 34 caves (Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain).
  • Kailash Temple (Ellora) was built by the Rashtrakuta dynasty.
  • Elephanta Caves were named by the Portuguese.
  • Khajuraho temples were built by the Chandela Rajputs.
  • Konark Sun Temple is also known as the 'Black Pagoda'.
  • Bruhdeshwar Temple was built by King Rajraja-I of the Chola dynasty.
  • Hampi was the capital of the Vijaynagar kingdom.
  • Agra Fort was built by Emperor Akbar in 1565 AD.
  • The Taj Mahal construction took 22 years to complete.
  • Fatehpur Sikri was founded by Akbar.
  • Rani ni Vav (Patan, Gujarat) was declared a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2014.
  • The local name for Elephanta Caves is 'Dhara puri'.
