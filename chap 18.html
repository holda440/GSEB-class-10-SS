Chapter 18 - Price Rise and Consumer Awareness

A. Understanding Price Rise üìà

  • Price Rise:

    A continuous and constant increase in the prices of goods and services in different sectors of the economy at a high rate.

  • Stable Price Rise:

    A gradual and stable rise in price is considered beneficial for the economy as it encourages producers to invest and expand, leading to increased production and employment.

  • Inflationary Situation:

    A condition where the money supply increases speedily, but the production of goods and services does not. This leads to "too much money chasing too few goods," causing a sharp and continuous price rise, which hinders economic development.

B. Reasons for Price Rise üßê

‚ö´ Two main factors: (a) Lack of instantaneous increase in production/supply, and (b) Sharp increase in total demand.

1. Increase in Monetary Supply: Money supply increases via (i) deficit financing (printing new money), (ii) increased circulation of money, and (iii) low interest rates on loans. This boosts purchasing power and demand without a corresponding increase in supply.

2. Population Growth üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß‚Äçüë¶: India's population growth (1.9% annually) creates an imbalance where demand for essential goods constantly outstrips supply.

3. Increase in Exports: When the government encourages exports, the availability of those goods in the domestic market decreases, creating scarcity and pushing prices up.

4. High Cost of Raw Materials: Shortages or increased prices of raw materials raise production costs, which are then passed on to consumers as higher prices for finished goods.

5. Black Money üí∞: Unregistered income (black money) is often spent quickly on non-essential items to avoid taxes, creating artificial demand and supporting price rise.

6. Price Rise by Government: The government increases prices of petroleum products, fertilizers, and agricultural support prices, which has a cascading effect on the prices of other commodities.

7. Natural & Human Factors: Natural calamities (drought, flood) and man-made factors (war, strikes) disrupt production and supply chains, leading to shortages and price hikes.

8. Hoarding, Smuggling & Black Marketing: These illegal activities create artificial scarcity. Hoarders stock up on goods expecting future price rises, and black marketers sell them at exorbitant prices. This is also called profiteering.

C. Why Control Price Rise and How? ‚öñÔ∏è

  • Adverse Effects of Price Rise:
    • Decreases savings and capital generation.
    • Deteriorates the living standards of the poor and middle class.
    • Reduces exports (as they become expensive) and increases imports.
    • Hinders production of necessary goods.
    • Promotes immoral activities like hoarding, black marketing, and corruption.
  • Steps to Control Price Rise:
    • 1. Monetary Measures (by RBI):
      • Reduce money circulation by increasing bank interest rates (making loans expensive).
      • Encourage savings by offering higher interest on deposits.
      • Increase bank reserve funds to control lending capacity.
      • Sell government securities to absorb excess cash from the market.
    • 2. Fiscal Measures (by Government):
      • Reduce its own expenditure and postpone non-essential projects.
      • Increase taxes (income, company, property tax) to reduce public spending power.
      • Launch public loan policies and savings schemes (e.g., Compulsory Saving Plan) to absorb excess money.
    • 3. Control on Capital Investment:
      • Use licensing/permit systems to discourage investment in luxury items and encourage investment in essential goods.
    • 4. Price Regulation & Rationing (PDS):
      • The Public Distribution System (PDS) provides essential commodities at reasonable prices to the poor through Fair Price Shops (FPS).
    • 5. Administered Price Mechanism:
      • The government fixes the prices of essential goods under the **Essential Commodities Act, 1955**, and takes legal action against hoarders and black marketers.

D. Consumer Awareness: Jago Grahak Jago! üõçÔ∏è

  • Consumer Protection:

    A system and movement ensuring that consumers receive goods and services of good quality, proper weight, and at a reasonable price, protecting them from exploitation.

  • Reasons for Consumer Exploitation:

    1. Consumer is responsible: Ignorance, illiteracy, and lack of unity among consumers make them easy targets.

    2. Limited Information: Producers do not provide complete information about product quality, use, and maintenance.

    3. Limited Supply: Artificial scarcity created by hoarding leads to over-pricing.

    4. Limited Competition: In a monopoly, a single producer can exploit consumers with high prices and low-quality goods.

  • Key Dates & Acts:
    • **15th March:** World Consumer Rights Day.
    • **24th December:** National Consumer Right Day (India).
    • **Consumer Protection Act, 1986:** A milestone act in India for protecting consumer rights. Signed on 24th December 1986.

E. Rights and Duties of the Consumer üõ°Ô∏è

  • The Six Consumer Rights:
    üß† **Mnemonic: SIC-RRE** üß†
    Remember your rights with "SIC-RRE"!
    **S**afety, **I**nformation, **C**hoice, **R**epresentation, **R**edressal, **E**ducation
    • **Right to Safety:** Protection from goods and services that are hazardous to health and life.
    • **Right to be Informed:** The right to have information about the quality, quantity, purity, price, etc.
    • **Right to Choose:** The right to access a variety of goods at competitive prices.
    • **Right to be Heard/Represent:** The right to have consumer interests considered in relevant forums.
    • **Right to Seek Redressal:** The right to compensation against unfair trade practices or exploitation.
    • **Right to Consumer Education:** The right to acquire knowledge and skills to be an informed consumer.
  • Duties of the Consumer:
    • Make wise choices; check for quality marks (ISI, Agmark).
    • Check labels, manufacturing/expiry dates, and other information.
    • Always insist on an original bill and a filled warranty card.
    • Do not compromise on quality and safety.
    • Form or join consumer organizations to fight exploitation collectively.
    • File a complaint against any malpractice.
    • Purchase only what is necessary and avoid wastage.

  • 2. Consumer Societies (Consumer Protection Councils):

    Voluntary, non-political organizations that create awareness, publish magazines (e.g., "Grahak Suraksha"), and help consumers file cases.

  • 3. Public Distribution System (PDS):

    Saves poor consumers from exploitation in the open market by providing essential goods at subsidized rates.

  • 4. Certification and Marks of Quality:
    • **ISI/BIS:** For industrial and consumer goods (textiles, electronics, cement).
    • **AGMARK:** For agricultural, forest, and horticultural products.
    • **Hallmark (BIS):** For gold jewellery (e.g., 916 for 22-carat gold).
    • **FPO (Food Product Order):** For processed fruit products like jams and juices.
    • **Woolmark:** For woolen products.
    • **MPO:** For meat and mutton products.
    • **HACCP:** For processed food, ensuring safety standards.
    • **ECO:** For environment-friendly products (soap, paper, detergents).
  • 5. International Organizations:
    • **ISO (International Organization for Standardization):** Geneva-based org that provides international standards (e.g., ISO-9000).
    • **CAC (Codex Alimentarius Commission):** Rome-based org (est. by FAO & WHO) that develops food standards.

G. Filing a Complaint üìù

  • Who can file?

    A consumer, any registered consumer association, Central/State Govt., or a representative on behalf of consumers.

  • How to file?
    • Complaint can be handwritten, typed, or emailed.
    • Include name, address, contact details.
    • Provide a detailed description of the complaint and the reason.
    • Attach copies (not originals) of supporting documents: bills, receipts, warranty cards, advertisements, etc.
    • Lodge the complaint within **2 years** of the cause of action.
  • Helpline Numbers:

    Gujarat State: 1800-233-0222 | National Level: 1800-11-4000
