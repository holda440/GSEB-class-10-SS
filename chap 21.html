  • The Right to Constitutional Remedies is called the "Soul of the Constitution" as it allows citizens to seek justice from the High Court or Supreme Court if their rights are violated.

üî∂ C. Social Issues and Protection Measures

‚ú® Child Labour and Protection Against Exploitation

  • Child Abuse: Any deliberate or accidental act that injures a child, including physical punishment, threatening, abusive language, or sexual harassment.
  • Child Labour: A global problem involving labourers below 14 years of age. India has the highest number of child labourers. They are found in hotels, factories, construction, agriculture, and as domestic help.

üìå Reasons for Child Labour

‚ú® Protection for the Elderly and Helpless üë¥üëµ

  • The average life span in India has increased, leading to a larger elderly population and associated social/physical problems.
  • Due to western culture and nuclear families, many elderly face neglect and are forced into Old Age Homes.

üìå Key Initiatives and Laws

  • UN Initiatives: Declared 1999 as "International Elderly Year" and 1st October as "World Elderly People Day".
  • Government Schemes: "National Policy for the senior citizens" (1999), pension schemes (Indira Gandhi National Senior Citizen Pension Scheme), higher interest on deposits, travel concessions.
  • Legal Protection: "Parents and Senior Citizens Care and Welfare related law, 2007" protects them from domestic violence and abuse, and ensures they can get maintenance from their children.

‚ú® Anti-social Activities and Corruption

  • Anti-social Activity: Any individual or group behaviour against the laws of society.
  • Corruption: The misuse of a public position for personal benefits. Both the giver and the taker are guilty. It leads to degradation of moral values, black money, and loss of trust in the system.

üìå Steps to Curb Corruption

  • Central Anti-bribery Bureau (1964): Investigates allegations against government officials.
  • Anti Corruption Act, 1988: Aims to provide clean public life.
  • Other Acts: Right to Information Act (2005), Black Money Act (2005), FEMA, Money Laundering Act.
  • Institutions: Appointment of Lokpal and Lokayukt.

üî∂ D. Key Legislations for Social Reform

‚ú® Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005

  • Enacted on 15th June 2005 to promote transparency and accountability in administration.
  • Any citizen can request information about government departments, schemes, or pending work by paying a nominal fee (Rs. 20). BPL families are exempt.

‚ú® Right to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009 üìö

  • Based on the 86th Constitutional Amendment, it makes primary education (6-14 years) a fundamental right.
FeatureDescription
AdmissionGuaranteed admission in a local school for every child (6-14 years). No birth certificate needed if other proof is available.
Forbidden PracticesNo capitation fees, no admission tests/interviews for children or parents.
ReservationCompulsory 25% admission for children from weaker sections (BPL) in private primary schools (Std 1).
Teacher NormsTeachers are not allowed to take private tuitions. Under-qualified teachers must gain qualifications within 5 years.
No ExpulsionA child cannot be terminated from school until primary education is complete.
