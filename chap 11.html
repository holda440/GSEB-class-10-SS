A. The Essence of Water üíß
  • The text starts with a powerful quote: 'There is water hence there is a life.'
  • Life on Earth is unimaginable without water; it's an inseparable part of the environment and life.
  • A nation's prosperity, especially in agriculture, depends heavily on its water resources.
  • Water is essential for drinking, domestic use, and industries.

Feature Description
Rain Water This is the 'original' or primary source of all water on Earth. All other sources like rivers, lakes, and wells exist because of rainfall.
Surface Water Water found on the Earth's surface in rivers, lakes, ponds, and streams. Rivers are the main source of surface water in India.
Ground Water Water that is absorbed by the land and stored in the lower strata. It's a significant source, especially for irrigation. The Northern plains have abundant ground water (42%), while Southern India has less due to its plateau and mountainous terrain.

D. The Growing Water Crisis üò•
  • The crisis is intensifying due to:
    • Increasing population and demand for food grains.
    • Shift to water-intensive cash crops.
    • Rapid urbanization and changing lifestyles.
  • The crisis is most alarming in arid regions like Western Rajasthan and the interior southern plateau.
  • Water quality has deteriorated, leading to the spread of water-borne diseases.
  • There's a severe shortage of drinking water, with about 8% of towns in India facing this issue and about 50% of villages needing access to clean water.
  • Groundwater levels are falling due to excessive extraction, especially in states like Punjab and Haryana.
E. Management and Conservation of Water ‚ôªÔ∏è
Watershed Development
  • A watershed is a natural area where all water drains into a single river basin.
  • Development involves a holistic approach:
    • Soil and humidity conservation.
    • Water storage, tree plantation, and horticulture.
    • Developing pastureland.
  • It's a community-driven approach, supported by state and central governments.

F. Case Study: Bedaj Village Success Story üå±
  • Villagers in Bedaj (Aravalli district) solved their water scarcity by conserving 2.25 crore litres of rainwater.
  • They formed an association, deepened the village pond, and built 10 check dams, 11 farm ponds, and other structures.
  • Results: Dry land turned green, milk production increased by 12.5%, water storage by 20-30%, and overall farm production rose.
Quick Review: One-Mark Facts üéØ
  • 'Rain' is the original source of water on Earth.
  • Rivers are the main source of surface water.
  • Northern plains of India hold about 42% of the ground water.
  • About 84% of water in India is used for irrigation.
  • Producing 1 kg of wheat requires about 1500 litres of water.
  • The Grand Anicut canal was built on the Kaveri river in the 2nd century.
  • The Eastern Yamuna Canal was built in 1882 in Uttar Pradesh.
  • Wells and tube wells are the major mediums of irrigation in India.
  • After Independence, the irrigated area in India has increased four times.
  • Irrigation is practiced in about 38% of the net sown area.
  • Punjab has the highest percentage of irrigated area (90.8%), while Mizoram has one of the lowest (7.3%).
  • About 8% of towns in India face a severe shortage of drinking water.
