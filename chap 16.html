16 - Economic Liberalization and Globalization

Post-independence in 1947, India adopted economic planning (Five Year Plans) for rapid development. However, due to limited success, the government investigated the failures and introduced new economic policies in 1991 to reform the economy.

A. Economic Liberalization üïäÔ∏è

The process where the government gradually reduces restrictions and controls on the private sector to encourage economic development.

Key Reforms Undertaken:
  • Reservation for industries reduced from 18, eliminating the need for government permission for most sectors.
  • All areas except Railways, Atomic Energy, and Defence were opened for the private sector.
  • The mandatory registration requirement for industries was cancelled.
  • Permission for setting up non-polluting and non-hazardous industries was abolished.
Advantages of Liberalization üëç
  • Unconstrained Growth: Private sector gained the freedom to develop, leading to increased production.
  • Global Trade Boost: Acceptance of liberalization spurred global trade.
  • Foreign Exchange Reserves: Increased foreign trade led to a higher reserve of foreign currency.
  • Infrastructure Development: Internal infrastructure facilities improved significantly.
Disadvantages of Liberalization üëé
  • Monopoly Issues: Despite reduced restrictions, monopolies could not be effectively controlled.
  • Agricultural Neglect: The primary focus remained on the industrial sector, leaving agriculture underdeveloped.
  • Income Disparity: The gap between the rich and the poor widened.
  • Increased Foreign Debt: Imports grew faster than exports, leading to an increase in foreign debt.
B. Privatization üíº

The process of transferring ownership and management of state-controlled industrial units to the private sector.

Methods of Privatization:

Feature: Opening up reserved sectors.
Description: Areas previously reserved for the public sector are made accessible to private companies.

Feature: Disinvestment / Management Transfer.
Description: The state either transfers management to a private company while retaining ownership, or vice-versa, transferring ownership while keeping management.

Advantages of Privatization üëç
  • More Production Units: Led to an increase in the number of industrial units.
  • Higher Production: Increased output of capital-intensive and consumer goods.
  • Improved Public Sector: The efficiency and working capacity of public sector enterprises improved through competition.
Disadvantages of Privatization üëé
  • Economic Centralization: Led to the concentration of economic power and the rise of monopolies.
  • Neglect of Small Industries: Cottage and small-scale industries did not develop, as large industries reaped most benefits.
  • Price Rise: Lack of price controls led to inflation.
C. Globalization üåç

The process of connecting a country's economy with the world economy, allowing for the easy flow of goods, services, technology, and labor across borders.

Key Improvements due to Globalization:
  • Removal of trade barriers between nations.
  • Creation of an environment for easy capital exchange.
  • Removal of obstacles to technology transfer.
  • Facilitation of free movement of labor.
Effects on Indian Economy (Mixed Bag) üìä
  • Advantage - FDI: Encouraged Foreign Direct Investment (FDI).
  • Advantage - Access to Goods: Goods from developed countries became easily available.
  • Advantage - Competitiveness: Enabled India to compete at an international level.
  • Disadvantage - Poverty/Unemployment: Did not achieve expected success in solving these issues.
  • Disadvantage - Unequal Export Benefits: Developing countries did not gain the full benefits of increased exports.
  • Disadvantage - Industry Disparity: Large-scale industries benefited more than small-scale industries.
D. World Trade Organisation (WTO) üèõÔ∏è

An international organization established on January 1, 1995, with its headquarters in Geneva, Switzerland. It aims to regulate and facilitate international trade.

Remembering WTO Objectives with "RRCS"

R - Remove trade obstacles.
R - Remove protectionist policies.
C - Co-ordinate global trade policy.
S - Solve trade disputes.

Impact on Indian Economy üáÆüá≥
  • Increased Trade Share: India's contribution to world trade increased to over 1%.
  • Export Growth: Exports of readymade clothes and agricultural products increased.
  • Reduced Import Pressure: Higher exports and foreign investment reduced pressure from imports.
E. Sustainable Development üå±

Development that meets the needs of the present generation without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

Key Strategies for Conservation & Sustainable Development

üåø Use renewable resources wisely & non-renewable resources judiciously.
üè≠ Promote "Eco-friendly development" (e.g., plan industrial locations to reduce transport costs).
üíß Utilize resources for multiple purposes (e.g., dams for irrigation, electricity, flood control).
‚ôªÔ∏è Prevent waste and control pollution.

F. Steps Taken to Protect Environment üõ°Ô∏è
  • Global Action: The "Earth Conference" in Stockholm, Sweden (1972) was a landmark event for global environmental protection.
  • India's National Efforts:
    • Establishment of Central and State Pollution Control Boards.
    • Declaration of June 5th as "Environment Day".
    • Enactment of the "Air Pollution Control Act" in 1981.
    • Signing international agreements on ozone depletion, nuclear waste, and biodiversity.
