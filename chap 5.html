5 - India's Heritage of Science and Technology

A. Introduction to Science and Technology in Ancient India

Science means systematic 'Knowledge' and Technology means 'the practical utility of systematic knowledge'.

Ancient India made outstanding contributions in Metallurgy, Chemistry, Medicine, Surgery, Mathematics, Astronomy, Astrology, and Vastushastra.

Modern research proves that ancient India had a scientific attitude combined with a religious outlook.

B. Metallurgy ‚öíÔ∏è

Features of Ancient Indian Metallurgy

  • Early Evidence: Use of metallurgy dates back to the Indus Valley Civilization.
    • A metallic idol of a female dancer is a prime example.
  • Skilled Artistry: Statues from various periods showcase exceptional skill.
    • Kushan Period: Statues of Buddha found at Takshshila.
    • Chola Period: Numerous metal idols, including the world-famous Natraja statue (masterpiece of sculpture, preserved in Chennai).
    • Other Examples: 'Ram - The archer' idol, statues of Gods-Goddesses, birds, animals, and intricate betel-nut cutters.

C. Chemistry (Alchemical Lore) üß™

Chemistry is an experimental science useful for minerals, plants, agriculture, metal works, and medicine.

Pioneers and Institutions

Feature Description
Acharya Nagarjuna A learned Buddhist of Nalanda University, known as an 'Acharya' of Chemistry. He wrote 'Rasaratnakar' and 'Arogyamanjari'. He advocated using Alopathy with herbal medicines and initiated the use of mercury ash as medicine.
Nalanda University Had its own dedicated school of chemistry and furnaces for research. Chemistry texts from here describe main `rasa`, `uprasa`, ten types of poisons, salts, and ashes of minerals.

Remarkable Chemical Feats

  • Copper Statue of Buddha (Sultangunj): 7.5 feet high and weighs one tonne.
  • Buddha Statue (Nalanda): 18 feet high.
  • Vijay Stambha (Iron Pillar, Delhi): A 24-feet high, 7-tonne iron pillar built by Chandragupta II. It has not corroded over centuries, showcasing advanced alchemical knowledge.

Advanced Surgical and Medical Practices

  • Advanced Techniques: Could stop blood circulation with bowl-shaped bandages and perform complex operations (abdomen, kidney, cataract, hernia, piles).
  • Orthopedics: Skillful in joining broken or displaced bones.
  • Plastic Surgery: Had knowledge of joining nose and ears.
  • Medical Education: Taught students surgery on dead bodies or wax statues.
  • Specializations: Were expert Gynaecologists and Paediatricians.
  • Veterinary Science: Developed veterinary science with books like 'Hasti Ayurveda' (for elephants) and 'Ashwashastra' (for horses).

F. Astronomy and Astrology üî≠‚ú®

Astronomy is the most ancient science in India, with deep studies conducted in ancient universities.

Major Astronomers and their Work

  • Aryabhatta: Made remarkable contributions.
    • Declared that the Earth rotates on its own axis.
    • Correctly explained that the lunar eclipse is caused by the shadow of the Earth.
    • India's first satellite was named 'Aryabhatta' in his honor.
  • Brahmgupta: Popularized the laws of gravitation in his book 'Brahmasiddhant'.
  • Varahmihir: A great astrologer and astronomer.
    • Divided astrology into 3 sections: Tantra, Hora, and Samhita.
    • Wrote 'Brihadsamhita', which covers the effects of planets on humans, marriage, farming, etc.

G. Vastushastra (Science of Architecture) üèõÔ∏è

Vastushastra is an inseparable part of astrology and an ancient Indian science of construction and architecture, now recognized worldwide.

Pioneers and Principles

  • Pioneers: Brahma, Narad, Bruhspati, Bhrugu, Vashishtha, and Vishwakarma are considered the main contributors.
  • Vishwakarma: Regarded as the "first architect of the Gods," he divided Vastushastra into eight sections.
  • Principles Covered: Selection of place, shapes, structure, planning of dwellings, temples, palaces, forts, and town planning.
  • Revival: Rana Kumbha of Mewar revived this science in the 15th century.
