What is Heritage? A Quick Look

Heritage is what we inherit from our ancestors. It can be divided into three main types:

  • üß¨ Genetic Heritage: Physical and mental traits from parents.
  • üè† Material Heritage: Property like house, land, and estates.
  • üé® Cultural Heritage: Anything a person creates with intellect and skill, including traditions, customs, rituals, arts, and skills. India's culture includes 64 distinct arts!
üî∏ Art of Indian Artisans
  • The beauty of India's cultural heritage lies in the traditional art of its artisans.
  • They have skillfully developed a varied lifestyle for people through remarkable art and handicraft industries.
B. Traditional Indian Handicrafts üè∫
‚ú® Clay Work

Feature: One of the most ancient human crafts, associated from cradle to grave.

Description:

  • Pre-Metal Era: Abundantly used for toys, pots, pitchers, earthen lamps, and chulhas (clay stoves).
  • Kitchen Wares: Most kitchen items were made of clay to store grains, milk, ghee, curd, etc.
  • Historical Evidence: Red-coloured clay utensils found at Lothal, Mohan-Jo-Daro, and Harappa.
  • Key Technology: The potter's wheel is considered the best ancient device for clay work.
  • Modern Relevance: Seen in the 'Garba' (a holed pitcher with a lamp inside) during Navratri. Baked (terracotta) utensils from Nagarjunakonda and Laghnaj also provide evidence of this art.
‚ú® Art of Weaving

Feature: The art of creating fabric from threads, a cornerstone of India's self-reliance movement.

Description:

  • Spinning: The art of making long threads from cotton swabs, championed by Gandhiji for the Swadeshi movement.
  • Hand Weaving: India's ancient textile know-how is famous. A fine muslin cloth from Dhakha could pass through a ring and fit in a matchbox!

The famous saying goes: 'Padi patole bhat, fate pan fite nahi' ‚Äì The design laid on a Patola will never fade, even if the cloth tears.
‚ú® Art of Embroidery

Feature: Decorating cloth with needlework, a tradition seen since the Indus Valley Civilization.

Description:

  • Ancient Roots: Seen on idols from Harappa and Mohan-Jo-Daro. Found in Sindh, Saurashtra, and Kuchchha regions.
  • Famous Styles: Kashmiri (Kasab) work is well-known. Bandhani of Jamnagar, Jetpur, Bhuj, and Mandvi is popular for its beautiful designs of elephants, puppets, and pitchers.
  • Unique Work: The embroidery of the 'Jat' community in the Banni area of Kutch is unique.
‚ú® Leather Industry

Feature: Processing the skin of dead animals for various uses.

Description:

  • Ancient Uses: Used for 'Masak' (water bags), 'Kos' (for drawing water), musical instruments (dholak, tabla), bellows for blacksmiths, and shields for war.
  • Famous Products: Well-knitted 'Mojadi' of Rajasthan, footwear, belts, and items for horses and camels (saaj, palan).
‚ú® Diamond, Bead, and Enamel Work

Feature: Expertise in gems and jewellery, leveraging India's long coastline (7517 km).

Description:

  • Diamonds: World-famous diamonds like 'Kohinoor' and 'Great Mughal' were found in India. Diamond-studded ornaments have long been in demand.
  • Bead Work: Prominent in Gujarat, used to create beautiful torans, pots, 'chakda' (wall pieces), and decorative coconuts.
  • Enamel Work (Meenakari): The artistic skill of inlaying bright colours like red, green, and blue onto gold and silver ornaments. Key centers include Jaipur, Delhi, Lucknow, and Varanasi.
‚ú® Jari Work (Zari)

Feature: Weaving with golden and silver threads to decorate clothes.

Description:

  • Prominent Center: Surat is a leading hub for Jari work.
  • Artisanship: Artisans of Surat are skilled in making beautiful dresses like sarees, panetar, and gharchola bordered with Jari. Jardoshi work also has a unique beauty.
‚ú® Metal Work

Feature: An old art form that developed after the Stone Age.

Description:

  • Lothal Artisans: Made weapons like sickles, drills, and needles from copper and bronze.
  • Material Uses: Gold/Silver for ornaments, Copper/Brass/Bronze for utensils and idols, and Iron for instruments and weapons.
‚ú® Wooden Art

Feature: The art of carving and constructing with wood.

Description:

  • Evolution: Initially used as firewood, later for weapons, construction, and finally for idols, toys, and furniture.
  • Famous Products: Wooden furniture like swings and toys from Idar and Sankheda in Gujarat are well-known.
‚ú® Inlay Work

Feature: An ancient art of studding ornaments with precious gems.

Description:

  • Use: Royalty wore ornaments like necklaces, crowns, and rings studded with diamonds, pearls, and rubies.
  • Popular Region: The Bikaner area of Rajasthan is famous for inlay ornaments.
‚ú® Akik Work (Agate)

Feature: Crafting ornaments from a precious chalcedonic stone.

Description:

  • The Stone: Akik is mainly chalcedonic mixed with silica (blue or white). Carnelian is a red, semi-transparent variety.
  • Process: Stones are found in Gujarat (Ranpur, Surat) and sent to Khambhat to be moulded into beautiful ornaments like beads and garlands.

‚ú® Painting

Feature: The foremost art, expressing emotions through lines and colours.

Description:

  • History: Evidence from 5000-year-old Harappan culture.
  • Primitive Art: Cave paintings of animals and birds at Bhimbetka (M.P.).
  • Masterpieces: Ajanta-Ellora paintings are incomparable specimens.
  • Traditions: Drawing Swastik, Pitcher, and Ganesha during festivals, and Rangoli making are old Indian traditions.
C. Fine Arts of India üé≠üéµüíÉ
üéµ Music

Feature: Unique in tune, rhythm, and harmony, with roots in the Samveda.

Description:

  • The Seven Vowels (Swaras): SA, RE, GA, MA, PA, DHA, NI.
  • Main Types: Classical Music and Folk Music.
  • Classical Ragas: Believed to be six main ragas - Shree, Deepak, Hindol, Megh, Bhairavi, and Malkauns.
  • Key Texts:
    • Sangeet Makarand: Written by Narad (900 BC), describes 19 veenas and 101 rhythms.
    • Sangeet Ratnakar: Written by Pt. Sarangdev, considered the most authentic book, covering both North and South Indian music.
    • Sangeet Parijat: Written by Pt. Ahobale (1665), describes 29 types of tunes.
  • Famous Musicians: Amir Khushroo (Tuti-e-Hind), Bhakti saints, Tansen, and the Tana-Riri sisters.
üíÉ Dance

Feature: A medium for realizing beauty, with Lord Shiva ('Nataraj') as its originator.

Description: India has a wide variety of classical dances.

Remembering India's Classical Dances!

Think: "Bharat Keeps Kool Kathas for Manipur & Odisha"

  • B - Bharatnatyam (Tamil Nadu)
  • K - Kuchipudi (Andhra Pradesh)
  • K - Kathakali (Kerala)
  • K - Kathak (North India)
  • M - Manipuri (Manipur)
  • O - Odissi (Odisha - mentioned in text as a classical dance)
  • Bharatnatyam: Originates from Tanjor, Tamil Nadu. Sources are 'Natyashastra' and 'Abhinav Darpan'.
  • Kuchipudi: From Andhra Pradesh, focuses on describing women's beauty.
  • Kathakali: Famous in Kerala, based on epics. Known for beautiful attires and intricate facial expressions.
  • Kathak: The name comes from "Kathan kare so Kathak kahave" (One who tells a story is a Kathak). Narrates events from Lord Krishna's life.
  • Manipuri: Based on the Raslila of Shri Krishna. The green petticoat worn is called 'Kumin'.
üé≠ Dramatic Art

Feature: A unique art that presents 'sanskar' (values) with entertainment.

Description:

  • Source Text: 'Natyashastra' by Bharatmuni is the key text. Bharatmuni said, "There is no book, there is no sculpture, there is no knowledge, there is no deed which is lacking in dramatic art."
  • Famous Dramatists: Bhas and Kalidas (Sanskrit), and 'Jayshankar Sundari' in Gujarati drama.
üìå Bhavai

Feature: A special, emotion-oriented Gujarati folk drama.

Description:

  • Origin: Started 700 years ago by Asait Thakar.
  • Style: Performed without curtains, with light humour and trumpet music.
  • Theme: Protests social vices and promotes social causes like girls' education (using characters like Rangla-Rangli).
D. Folk Dances of Gujarat ü•Å
üî∏ Tribal Dances
  • Performed in a circle to please Gods and Goddesses during festivals and fairs.
  • Chado Dance: Dancers imitate bird creatures like the peacock and squirrel.
  • Tippani Dance: Performed by Bhil and Koli labourers, using thick sticks.
üî∏ Garba
  • Origin: The word comes from 'Garbh Deep' (an earthen lamp inside a holed pot).
  • Performance: Dancers move in a circle around a 'Mandvi' (a shrine to Goddess Jagdamba) during Navratri.
  • Garbi: A form associated with Krishna Bhakti, made popular by poet Dayaram.
üî∏ Rasa
  • A dance performed in a circle with singing.
  • Origin Story: Lord Krishna showed 'Rasa Lila' to the devotee Narsinh Mehta.
  • Dandiya Rasa is one of its famous types.
üî∏ Other Dances of Gujarat
  • Gof Gunthan Dance: Dancers hold a rope in one hand and a dandiya in the other, weaving a plait (gunthan) around a pillar.
  • Dhamaal Dance of Siddis: Performed by the Siddi community (originally from Africa). They strike a 'Mashira' (a coconut shell instrument) and make a 'Ho-Ho' sound.
  • Padhar Dance: Dancers from the Padhar community imitate sea waves.
