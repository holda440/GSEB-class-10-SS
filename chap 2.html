Chapter 2 - Cultural Heritage of India : Traditional Handicraft and Fine Arts

A. Introduction to Cultural Heritage üèõÔ∏è

  • Genetic Inheritance: Physical and mental traits inherited from parents.
  • Material Heritage: Properties like house, land, and estates obtained from ancestors.
  • Cultural Heritage: Anything a person obtains or creates with intellect and skill. This includes traditions, customs, rituals, life styles, education, farming, arts, handicrafts, and beliefs.
  • India's ancient literature mentions 64 arts, including handicraft, skills (kasab), drawing, music, and dance.
  • Yoga is a part of India's cultural heritage, with June 21 celebrated as 'World Yoga Day'.

B. Art of Indian Artisans üé®

üî∏ Clay Work (Pottery)

  • Man and clay have been associated since ancient times, from "cradle to the grave".
  • Used extensively before the invention of metal for toys, pots, pitchers, earthen lamps, and kitchen wares.
  • Walls of huts (kubas) were plastered with a paste of cowdung and clay.
  • Evidence of red-colored clay utensils from Lothal, Mohenjo-Daro, and Harappan culture.
  • The potter's wheel is considered the best device for ancient clay work.
  • A modern example is the 'Garba' (a holed pitcher with a lamp inside) used during Navratri.
  • India was famous for both baked (terracotta) and unbaked clay utensils. Remains found in Nagarjunakonda (South India) and Laghnaj (Mehsana, Gujarat).

üî∏ Art of Weaving & Embroidery üßµ

Feature Description
Spinning The art of making long threads from small pieces of cotton. Gandhiji promoted it as a cottage industry under the Swadeshi movement.
Hand Weaving India is known for its textile know-how. Fine muslin from Dhakha was so delicate that a whole piece could pass through a ring.
Patola of Patan ‚ú® World-famous silk ware (Bevad Ikt) from Patan, Gujarat. An 850-year-old art that flourished during the Solanki era. It is a double-ikat weave, meaning it can be worn on either side. Known for its durability and vibrant colors that never fade. The saying goes: ‚ÄòPadi Patole Bhat, Phate Pan Fite Nahi‚Äô (The design on a Patola may tear, but it will never fade).
Embroidery üìå Found on idols from Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro. Famous styles include Kashmiri (Kasab) work and the Bandhani of Gujarat (Jamnagar, Jetpur, Bhuj, Mandvi) featuring designs of elephants, puppets, and pitchers. The Jat community of Banni (Kutch) is known for its unique embroidery.

üî∏ Leather Industry üëû

  • Skin of dead animals was processed to make items like 'Masak' (water bag), 'kos', musical instruments (dholak, tabla), bellows for blacksmiths, footwear, and shields for war.
  • Famous products include Mojadi of Rajasthan, shoes, purses, and belts.

üî∏ Diamond, Bead, and Enamel Work üíé

  • India's long coastline (7517 km) provided plenty of gems and beads for trade.
  • World-famous diamonds like 'Kohinoor' and 'Great Mughal' were found in India.
  • Bead work is prominent in Gujarat, used to create 'Torans', pots, 'chakda' (wall pieces), and decorative coconuts.
  • Enamel work (Meenakari): The art of inlaying bright colors (red, green, blue) into gold and silver ornaments. Experts are found in Jaipur, Delhi, Lucknow, and Varanasi.

üî∏ Jari Work (Zardozi)

  • The art of weaving and embroidering with golden and silver threads to decorate clothes.
  • Surat is a prominent center for Jari work, creating beautiful sarees, panetars, and gharcholas.

üî∏ Metal Work (Engraving)

  • Artisans of Lothal made weapons (sickle, drill) and utensils from copper and bronze. Iron was used for instruments and weapons.

üî∏ Wooden Art (Wood Carving)

  • Wood was used for idols, toys, pillars, windows, doors, thrones, and chairs.
  • Wooden furniture like swings and toys from Idar and Sankheda in Gujarat are well-known.

üî∏ Inlay Work

  • Ancient art of studding ornaments (necklaces, crowns, rings) with precious gems like diamonds, pearls, and rubies.
  • The Bikaner area of Rajasthan is famous for inlay ornaments.

üî∏ Akik Work (Agate Work)

  • Akik is a type of precious stone (chalcedony mixed with silica) found in river valleys.
  • Carnelian, a red-colored stone, is a primary type.
  • Stones from Ranpur and Surat are sent to Khambhat (Cambay) to be molded into ornaments, beads, and garlands.

üî∏ Painting üñåÔ∏è

  • An art expressed through lines and colors, with evidence from the 5000-year-old Harappan culture.
  • Cave paintings of animals and birds by primitive man found at Bhimbetka (M.P.).
  • Ajanta-Ellora paintings are incomparable specimens.
  • Drawing Swastik, Pitcher, and Ganesha during festivals and making Rangoli are old traditions.

C. Fine Arts of India üé≠üéµüíÉ

üî∏ Music (Sangeet)

  • Indian music is unique in its tune, rhythm, and harmony. The Samveda is considered its origin.
  • It has seven vowels (swar): SA, RE, GA, MA, PA, DHA, NI.
  • Main types: Classical and Folk music.
  • Classical music has six main Ragas.

üß† Mnemonic for the 6 Ragas

Shree, Deepak, Hindol, Megh, Bhairavi, Malkauns

‚ú® Key Texts on Music:

Text Author & Details
Sangeet Makarand Written by music scholar Narad (around 900 BC). Describes 19 veenas and 101 rhythms.
Sangeet Ratnakar Written by Pandit Sarangdev (lived in Daulatabad). Considered the most authentic book on music, familiar with both North and South Indian styles.
Sangeet Parijat Written by Pandit Ahobale (1665) on Northern Hindustani music. He noted that each raga has its own individual identity.
  • Amir Khushroo: A famous musician and poet from the time of Allauddin Khilaji, known as 'Tuti-e-Hind' (Parrot of India).

üî∏ Dance (Nritya)

  • The word 'Nritya' comes from the Sanskrit 'Nrit'.
  • Lord Shiva ('Nataraj') is considered the originator of dance.

üáÆüá≥ India's Classical Dance Forms üíÉ

Dance Form Origin / Key Features
Bharatnatyam Originates from Tanjore, Tamil Nadu. Based on 'Natyashastra' (Bharatmuni) and 'Abhinav Darpan' (Nandikeshwar). Gopi Krishna, Mrinalini Sarabhai, Hema Malini are famous proponents.
Kuchipudi Created in the 15th century in Andhra Pradesh. Primarily based on the description of women's beauty.
Kathakali Famous in Kerala. Based on epics like Ramayana and Mahabharata. Known for elaborate, beautiful attires and artistic crowns. Performers use facial expressions to convey stories.
Kathak Based on the line 'Kathan kare so Kathak kahave'. It narrates the life events of Lord Krishna and developed with 'Shrungar Bhakti'. Famous artists include Pandit Birju Maharaj.
Manipuri From Manipur, performed on every occasion. Based on Raslila and Bal Lila of Shri Krishna. The plaited green petticoat ('Kumin') is a key part of the costume.

üî∏ Dramatic Art (Natya Kala)

  • Natyashastra by Bharatmuni is a well-known text. He states, "There is no book, no sculpture, no knowledge, no deed which is lacking in dramatic art."
  • Great Sanskrit dramatists include Bhas and Kalidas ('Abhigyan Shakuntalam').
  • In Gujarat, Jayshankar Sundari is a foremost name in dramatic art.
  • Bhavai: A special dramatic art of Gujarat, started 700 years ago by Asait Thakar. It's an emotion-oriented drama performed without curtains, using light humor and trumpets. It often includes social messages like promoting girls' education ('Rangla-Rangli').

üî∏ Folk Dances of Gujarat üï∫

Feature Description
Tribal Dances Performed at festivals and fairs. Dancers form a circle with traditional instruments. Examples: 'Chado' dance (imitating birds), 'Mali no Chaalo' & 'Thakryo Chaalo' (Dang), 'Tippani Dance' (Bhil and Koli communities).
Garba Derived from 'Garbh Deep' (earthen lamp in a holed pot). Performed during Navratri to worship Adhya Shakti. Dancers move in a circle around a 'Mandvi'.
Rasa A circular dance performed with singing. Lord Krishna showed 'Rasa Lila' to Narsinh Mehta. Dandiya Rasa is a popular form.
Other Dances Gof Gunthan: Dancers hold a rope and dandiya, forming and opening a plait (gunthan).
Dhamaal Dance of Siddis: Performed by the Siddi community (originally from Africa) in Jambur (Gir). They strike 'Mashira' (coconut shells) and imitate animal sounds.
Merayo, Padhar, Koli Dances: Regional dances with unique props and styles found in Banaskantha and Surendranagar regions.
